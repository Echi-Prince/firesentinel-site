The cost of delay

Traditional smoke alarms or human monitoring often detect fires too late. Cameras already capture early signs, but no one watches them 24/7 — leading to delayed responses, false alarms, and avoidable losses.

  • $50B+ annual fire damage globally
  • 15 min typical response time
  • 350K U.S. house fires each year

What success looks like

Early visual detection + instant alerts → faster dispatch, fewer losses, safer communities.

The solution: FireSentinel

AlexNet-based computer vision detects flames and smoke in live video feeds. When confidence crosses a safety threshold, it instantly notifies authorities.

Learning curves

Below are the actual W&B curves from multiple AlexNet and ResNet18 runs.

  • ResNet18 (bs32, lr=1e-4) achieved the best validation performance.
  • All AlexNet 1e-4 configs converged well (~0.9–0.96 val accuracy).
  • AlexNet lr=1e-3 was unstable: high loss + poor val accuracy.
  • Augmentation improved generalization and stability.
  • Future work: expand dataset, increase robustness, test harsh conditions.
